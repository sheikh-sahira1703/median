For finding the median of discrete series, make a new column in the data series called cumulative frequency and in that column, write the cumulative total of the frequency after each observation in the discrete series
Then find the value of (n+1)/2, if the value of cumulative frequency above the particular row is less than (n+1)/2 and the value of the cumulative frequency is more than (n+1)/2 for a column below that row, then the value of x in that row becomes the median

Quartiles are the observations that divide the data series into 4 parts

Inter Quartile Range

This is the interval that contains the middle 50% of the observations and it is given by Q3-Q1

Outliers

These are the values that are at the extreme upper or the extreme lower end of the data series and they affect our analysis
Lower outlier: anything below Q1 – 1.5*IQR
Upper outlier: anything above Q3 + 1.5*IQR

Demerits of median

Here are the demerits of using median as a descriptive statistic for calculations

  1. We can’t find combined median
  2. Median is not considering the values, it considers the position and not the values. Hence it is used for qualitative analysis (where we do not pay much attention to the value of the data points)
  3. We have different methods to calculate median so median can be different for different methods but mean is same
  4. Can’t be calculated for NA values

Median is a good descriptive statistic to replace the missing values with it in QUALITATIVE datasets
